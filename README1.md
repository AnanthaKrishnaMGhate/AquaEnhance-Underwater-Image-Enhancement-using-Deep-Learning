## 🎯 Project Objective

Underwater images often suffer from poor visibility, color degradation, low contrast, and scattering effects due to light absorption in water. AquaEnhance aims to restore underwater images using deep learning techniques, producing visually appealing and information-rich results suitable for research and real-world applications.

---

## 🔍 Problem Statement

Water absorbs light differently across wavelengths, causing:

- Loss of red color components
- Reduced visibility
- Low contrast
- Haze and scattering
- Blurred object boundaries

These degradations negatively impact underwater exploration, marine research, and computer vision applications.

---

## 💡 Solution

AquaEnhance utilizes a UNet-based deep neural network with a ResNet34 backbone to learn the mapping between degraded underwater images and their enhanced counterparts.

The model automatically:

- Restores natural colors
- Enhances contrast
- Improves visibility
- Preserves image details
- Reduces underwater haze

## 🏆 Why AquaEnhance?

Unlike traditional enhancement techniques that rely solely on handcrafted filters, AquaEnhance leverages deep learning to understand complex underwater degradation patterns and generate visually superior results with minimal user intervention.
